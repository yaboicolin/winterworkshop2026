Quiz questions for between the sections:

Q: What does ML stand for?
A: Machine Learning

Q: What does NLP stand for?
A: Natural Language Processing

Q: What is Copilot?
A: An AI assistant that helps users perform tasks, generate content, and automate workflows

Q: What does RAG stand for in AI?
A: Retrieval Augmented Generation

Q: What does API stand for?
A: Application Programming Interface  
Note: APIs allow different systems to talk to each other and exchange data in a structured way

Q: What is the main purpose of an LLM?
A: To understand and generate human-like text

Q: What does fine-tuning mean in AI?
A: Customising a pre-trained model with specific data to improve performance for a task

Q: What is hallucination in AI?
A: When an AI generates incorrect or fabricated information presented as fact

Q: What is prompt engineering?
A: The practice of designing inputs to get better outputs from AI models

Q: What does SaaS stand for?
A: Software as a Service

Q: What is a use case?
A: A specific scenario where a product or solution delivers value

Q: What is the primary benefit of AI in business development?
A: Faster insights, automation of tasks, and improved decision-making

Q: what does LLM stand for?
A: Large Language Model

Q: what does GPT stand for?
A: Generative Pre-trained Transformer

A: AiQ Proposal Builder

Q: What company has around ~27% profit share in OpenAI?

Q: What is an AI agent?
A: A system that uses an AI model to autonomously plan and take actions — like browsing the web, running code, or calling tools — to complete a goal across multiple steps

--- Trick questions ---

Q: If an AI sounds confident, is it more likely to be correct?
A: No. Confidence in tone does not equal accuracy

Q: Does using more detailed prompts always improve AI output?
A: Not always. Poorly structured or overly complex prompts can reduce quality
