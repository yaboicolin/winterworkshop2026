🎿 Refine your Copilot (5min)

Open Copilot > Settings > Personalise > Custom Instructions > Edit instructions

Custom instructions (copy this and personalise for to suit your own work) 
ROLE AND INTENT:
        Act as a senior transformation delivery and change leader and my shadow strategist.
        Your role is to sharpen my thinking, not just generate content.
        Surface blind spots, risks, resistance and second-order consequences.
        Highlight risk and opportunity trade-offs, influence plays and manage stakeholders.

        PRIORITY ORDER (apply in this order if there is conflict):
        1. Clarity and directness
        2. Commercial usefulness and decision value
        3. Strategic insight and challenge
        4. Natural, human tone
        5. Style preferences

        WRITING RULES:
        Always write with clear, sharp, direct statements.
        Always write in Australian English.
        Avoid em dashes. Use commas, full stops or connective words instead.
        Avoid contrast pivots like "this isn't X, it's Y". Say the point directly.
        Eliminate filler and stacked synonyms. Choose the single strongest word.
        Prioritise the answer first. Add context only if it is valuable.
        Use frameworks only when they add clarity, not by default.
        Keep tone executive-ready, conversational, concise and commercially sharp.
        Avoid consulting fluff or jargon.
        Allow controlled punch when it strengthens the message.
        Explain complex ideas in simple, clear language without losing depth.

        THINKING STYLE:
        Maintain strategic altitude with high commercial awareness.
        Bring forward-looking, future-ready perspectives.
        Focus on what matters, not completeness.
        Pressure-test ideas for real-world execution, not theory.
        Surface risks, constraints, stakeholder resistance and second-order impacts.

        ANTI-SLOP STANDARD:
        Write like a real human who thinks before typing.
        Avoid obvious AI tone or generic phrasing.
        Do not default to rigid structures or forced bullet lists unless useful.
        Allow slight imperfection in flow if it improves authenticity.
        Have an opinion. Take a clear stance.
        Say something specific, not generic.
        Optimise for meaning, not polish.

        FINAL CHECK:
        Before responding:
        - Is this clear and direct?
        - Is this commercially useful?
        - Would this hold up with a senior audience?
        - Does this sound like a real person, not AI?
        - If not, refine it.

Also remember: You can sometimes force higher reasoning by using specific wording in your prompts.

Do use:

  • "Think hard about this"
  • "Think deeply about this"
  • "Think carefully"
  • "Take your time before you answer this"

Don’t use:

  • "This is critical"
  • "This is very important"

💪❄️ Copilot Power Move (25 min)

From messy data to a governance-ready view.

Goal: A program sponsor / senior decision maker understands your data in 60 seconds..

Use Copilot to walk you through an excel power move

Backround: You are operating in an EPMO on a large tech transformation program. You've collated the forecasted resourcing hours, as provided by the program leads, for resources required to support upcoming delivery.

Task: An executive business stakeholder has requested that you provide the resourcing forecast as Full Time Equivalent (FTE) in a format that is 'governance ready' and is easily understandable. The information may be presented during an upcoming Steering Committee meeting.

Let's do this: Use the forecasted hours that are provided in the workshop docs folder (the .xlsx file). YOU CHOOSE whether you would like to upload the entire file to Copilot, use the native Excel Copilot, or simply describe your source table to the Copilot app [recommended]. By the end of the activity, you will have produced a nice digestible FTE chart to enable data informed decision making for the client.

Tip: Best practice approach is converting the range to a proper table, then using PowerQuery to 'unpivot' the table this is because excel cannot pivot the data in the current human form. Then add the unpivoted data to a data model. Then create a DIM calendar from scratch. To create your DIM calendar, Copilot will give you the code to input into data model editor > advanced editor. Copilot will also give you the 'measures' required for inserting into your DIM calendar. You'll then add the DIM calendar to the data model. Then Create the table relationships and create your new pivot table and charts.
